Combustion of Biphenyl

A sample of biphenyl \(((\ce{C6H5})_{2})\) weighing \(0.526g\) was ignited in a bomb calorimeter initially at \(25^{\circ}C\), producing a temperature rise of \(1.91K\). In a separate calibration experiment, a sample of benzoic acid (\(\ce{C6H5COOH}\)) weighing \(0.825g\) was ignited under identical conditions and produced a temperature rise of \(1.94K\). For benzoic acid, the heat of combustion at constant volume is known to be \(3226\frac{kJ}{mol}\) (that is, \(\Delta U=-3226\frac{kJ}{mol}\)). Use this information to determine the standard enthalpy of combustion of biphenyl.

Heat of Combustion

\(1.150g\) of sucrose goes through combustion in a bomb calorimeter. If the temperature rose from \(23.42^{\circ}C\text{to}27.64^{\circ}C\) and the heat capacity of the calorimeter is \(4.90\frac{kJ}{\circ C}\), then determine the heat of combustion of sucrose, \(\ce{C12H22O11}\) (in kJ per mole of \(\ce{C12H22O11}\)).
