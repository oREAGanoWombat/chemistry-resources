Example Problems

Strong Acid/Strong Base Titration

A titration is carried out for \(25.00mL\) of \(0.100M\ce{HCl}\) (strong acid) with \(0.100M\) of a strong base \(\ce{NaOH}\). Calculate the pH at these volumes of added base solution:

  1. \(0.00 mL\)
  2. \(12.50 mL\)
  3. \(25.00 mL\)
  4. \(37.50 mL\)

Weak Acid/Strong Base Titration

Consider the titration of \(25.00 mL\) of \(0.100 M \ce{CH3CO2H}\) with \(0.100 M \ce{NaOH}\). The reaction can be represented as: $$\ce{CH3CO2H + OH- -> CH3CO2- + H2O}$$

Calculate the pH of the titration solution after the addition of the following volumes of \(\ce{NaOH}\) titrant:

  1. \(0.00 mL\)
  2. \(12.50 mL\)
  3. \(25.00 mL\)
  4. \(37.50 mL\)
